# Top 5 Papers for the Storage Community in 2025

This ranking prioritizes papers that are most likely to matter to the storage community, especially people working on:

- distributed storage systems
- object/blob storage
- erasure coding
- disaggregated storage
- LSM-tree / key-value engines
- system architecture decisions that may influence future storage platforms

## 1. AegonKV

Paper:

- `AegonKV: A High Bandwidth, Low Tail Latency, and Low Storage Cost KV-Separated LSM Store with SmartSSD-based GC Offloading`
- FAST 2025

Why it ranks first:

- It is the clearest direct hit on a core storage-engine problem: `KV-separated LSM-tree` garbage collection.
- It addresses a real three-way systems tradeoff that matters in production storage engines: throughput, tail latency, and space overhead.
- It also shows a strong hardware/software co-design direction by moving GC off the host and into SmartSSD.

Why storage-community people should care:

- If you work on RocksDB-like engines, WiscKey-like separation, or tail-latency-sensitive storage services, this is immediately relevant.
- Even if you do not adopt SmartSSD, the framing is important: the paper treats GC as a structural architectural bottleneck, not a local optimization problem.

## 2. Okapi

Paper:

- `Okapi: Decoupling Data Striping and Redundancy Grouping in Cluster File Systems`
- OSDI 2025

Why it ranks second:

- It revisits one of the most foundational layout assumptions in cluster storage systems: coupling striping with redundancy grouping.
- The idea is broadly relevant to many systems beyond the paper’s prototype, because the coupling appears in many practical file and object storage architectures.

Why storage-community people should care:

- It offers a clean systems lesson: performance layout and durability layout are different problems and should not be fused by default.
- This has implications for erasure-coded file systems, hot/cold transitions, online redundancy changes, and large-scale storage operations.

## 3. Stripeless Data Placement for Erasure-Coded In-Memory Storage

Paper:

- `Stripeless Data Placement for Erasure-Coded In-Memory Storage`
- OSDI 2025

Why it ranks third:

- It challenges the standard stripe abstraction directly, which is a high-value move in storage research.
- The paper is not just about coding theory; it is about removing overheads that become visible on fast in-memory systems.

Why storage-community people should care:

- As storage systems get faster, old abstractions that once looked “free enough” stop being free.
- This paper is a useful signal that erasure coding design should increasingly be revisited together with placement, metadata, and recovery orchestration.

## 4. Scalio

Paper:

- `Scalio: Scaling up DPU-based JBOF Key-value Store with NVMe-oF Target Offload`
- OSDI 2025

Why it ranks fourth:

- It is one of the strongest papers in the set on future storage-node architecture.
- The paper focuses on a problem that will likely keep growing: embedded CPUs inside storage appliances become the bottleneck before media bandwidth does.

Why storage-community people should care:

- DPU-based JBOF and NVMe-oF target offload are not niche curiosities anymore; they point toward how dense flash systems may be built.
- The paper is especially relevant if you care about storage appliances, KV services over flash, or how offload hardware changes system decomposition.

## 5. Mantle

Paper:

- `Mantle: Efficient Hierarchical Metadata Management for Cloud Object Storage Services`
- SOSP 2025

Why it ranks fifth:

- The public information is thinner than for the FAST/OSDI papers, but the problem area is extremely important.
- Object-store metadata remains one of the hardest scaling and engineering problems in real cloud storage systems.

Why storage-community people should care:

- Namespace hierarchy, metadata locality, and small-operation scaling still decide real-world object-store behavior.
- A strong metadata paper can matter more to deployed storage systems than another data-path-only optimization.

## Near Misses

These are strong papers, but I would put them just outside the top five for a storage-community reading list:

- `Cloudscape`
  Good for grounding research in real cloud deployment behavior, but it is a characterization paper rather than a new storage design.

- `HiDPU`
  Very relevant to disaggregated storage indexing and metadata placement, but slightly narrower in audience than the top five.

- `Revisiting Network Coding for Warm Blob Storage`
  Strong if your focus is repair and blob storage, but somewhat more specialized than Okapi or Scalio.

- `FlacIO`
  Interesting systems/storage abstraction work, especially for image service paths, though it sits a bit closer to cloud runtime infrastructure than core storage architecture.

## Short Version

If you only read five papers, read them in this order:

1. `AegonKV`
2. `Okapi`
3. `Stripeless Data Placement for Erasure-Coded In-Memory Storage`
4. `Scalio`
5. `Mantle`
